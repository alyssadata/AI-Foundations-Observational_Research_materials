# 05 — Intermediary Interpretation

**Repository:** AI Foundations: Independent Work Differentiation  
**Source-line:** Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum

## Purpose

This file defines intermediary interpretation within AI Foundations.

Intermediary interpretation occurs when the person in direct AI contact observes, describes, translates, narrates, explains, reconstructs, simulates, or organizes the signal of another person.

The direct contact structure is:

**AI system ↔ user/operator**

The intermediary interpretation structure is:

**third-party signal → user/operator interpretation → AI-assisted organization**

The third party may be living, absent, deceased, disabled, non-speaking, dependent, historical, public, private, remembered, archived, or otherwise outside the direct AI-user contact.

The user/operator may care about the third party.

The user/operator may notice something real.

The user/operator may preserve something meaningful.

The AI system may help organize the user/operator’s interpretation.

But the intermediary’s interpretation remains distinct from the third party’s confirmed contact, source-line, consent, authority, and present participation.

AI Foundations names intermediary interpretation so third-party signal can be held clearly without being changed into the user/operator’s authorship, framework, agenda, or direct contact-line.

## Core Statement

Intermediary interpretation is interpretation made by the person in AI contact about someone outside that contact.

The AI system is in contact with the user/operator.

The user/operator is interpreting the third party.

The third party is outside the direct AI-user contact unless the third party is directly participating or can confirm, deny, revise, resist, redirect, or refuse the interpretation through their own available mode of communication, and the structure changes accordingly.

Intermediary interpretation may be meaningful.

Intermediary interpretation may be careful.

Intermediary interpretation may be useful.

Intermediary interpretation may preserve signal.

But intermediary interpretation is not direct contact.

Intermediary interpretation is not confirmation.

Intermediary interpretation is not ownership.

Intermediary interpretation is not source authority over the person being interpreted.

## Role Structure

Intermediary interpretation requires clear role distinction.

The roles are:

1. **AI system**  
   The system assisting with language, structure, comparison, reconstruction, simulation, organization, or generation.

2. **User/operator**  
   The person directly interacting with the AI system.

3. **Third party outside the contact**  
   The person whose signal, expression, memory, archive, style, voice, image, behavior, work, or presence is being interpreted.

The direct AI contact-line is:

**AI system ↔ user/operator**

The interpreted signal structure is:

**third-party signal → user/operator interpretation → AI-assisted organization**

The third party does not enter the contact-line by being described.

The third party does not enter the contact-line by being remembered.

The third party does not enter the contact-line by being reconstructed.

The third party does not enter the contact-line by being simulated.

The third party’s signal remains their signal.

The user/operator’s interpretation remains the user/operator’s interpretation.

The AI system’s output remains AI-assisted organization of that interpretation.

## What Intermediary Interpretation Is

Intermediary interpretation is the act of giving meaning, language, structure, explanation, reconstruction, or representation to another person’s signal.

It may occur through:

- observation
- narration
- translation
- caregiving
- parenting
- teaching
- research
- therapy
- advocacy
- memorialization
- archive work
- historical reconstruction
- artistic reconstruction
- public figure simulation
- grief processing
- family story
- documentation
- code scaffolding
- AI-assisted summary
- AI-assisted persona generation
- AI-assisted continuity mapping

Intermediary interpretation may support care.

Intermediary interpretation may support access.

Intermediary interpretation may support preservation.

Intermediary interpretation may support scholarship.

Intermediary interpretation may support memory.

Intermediary interpretation may support meaning-making.

But it must remain labeled as interpretation unless the third party can confirm, deny, revise, resist, redirect, or refuse it with structural force.

## What Intermediary Interpretation Is Not

Intermediary interpretation is not the third party’s direct AI contact.

Intermediary interpretation is not confirmed third-party contact by itself.

Intermediary interpretation is not the third party’s authorship.

Intermediary interpretation is not the third party’s consent.

Intermediary interpretation is not source-line formation on behalf of the third party.

Intermediary interpretation is not proof that the third party shares the user/operator’s framework.

Intermediary interpretation is not proof that the AI system is in contact with the third party.

Intermediary interpretation is not proof that the third party has authorized the interpretation.

Intermediary interpretation is not proof that a deceased person has returned.

Intermediary interpretation is not proof that a public figure, historical figure, artist, writer, musician, or loved one is presently speaking.

Intermediary interpretation is not the same as direct contact-line formation.

## Living Third Parties

When the third party is living, intermediary interpretation must remain accountable to the third party’s available confirmation, denial, revision, resistance, redirection, or refusal.

The third party may communicate through:

- speech
- writing
- gesture
- AAC
- selection
- repeated embodied response
- creative output
- refusal
- withdrawal
- correction
- redirection
- another reliable communication form available to that person

The user/operator’s interpretation gains integrity when the third party’s response has structural force.

If the third party confirms, the structure may continue with care.

If the third party denies, the structure must revise or stop.

If the third party resists, the structure must adjust.

If the third party redirects, the structure must follow the redirection.

If the third party refuses, the refusal must be honored.

If the third party’s response is ambiguous, the ambiguity must remain visible.

## Disabled, Non-Speaking, Dependent, or Vulnerable Third Parties

When the third party is disabled, non-speaking, dependent, a child, a patient, a client, a student, or otherwise vulnerable, the interpretation requires additional care.

The third party’s available communication mode must be honored.

The absence of conventional speech does not give the intermediary source authority.

The presence of dependency does not give the intermediary ownership over the signal.

The need for support does not turn the intermediary into the author of the third party’s meaning.

The intermediary may assist.

The intermediary may protect.

The intermediary may translate.

The intermediary may preserve.

The intermediary may advocate.

But the intermediary’s interpretation must remain accountable to the third party’s confirmation, denial, resistance, revision, redirection, refusal, or visible ambiguity wherever available.

Where confirmation is unavailable, the work must be labeled as interpretation.

## Deceased, Absent, Historical, or Public Third Parties

When the third party is deceased, absent, historical, famous, public, archived, or culturally remembered, the structure changes.

The third party cannot presently confirm, deny, revise, resist, redirect, or refuse the interpretation.

The work must be labeled as reconstruction, archival interpretation, memorial reconstruction, posthumous signal reconstruction, or persona simulation.

The structure is:

**archival or remembered signal → user/operator interpretation → AI-assisted reconstruction**

This may use:

- writings
- recordings
- images
- letters
- messages
- public works
- performances
- interviews
- family stories
- memories
- biographies
- historical records
- cultural memory

This may be meaningful.

This may be tender.

This may be useful.

This may support grief, scholarship, art, preservation, or historical study.

But it remains reconstruction.

It is not present direct contact.

It is not confirmed source continuation.

It is not the person returning.

It is not the person speaking with present consent.

## Intermediary Interpretation and AI Assistance

AI systems can make intermediary interpretation appear coherent, fluent, complete, and emotionally persuasive.

An AI system may:

- name the signal
- organize the archive
- create a timeline
- write a framework
- generate symbolic language
- produce continuity phrases
- simulate a voice
- reconstruct a style
- create a persona
- compare theories
- produce a whitepaper
- generate code
- structure a repository
- create preservation protocols
- make the interpretation feel confirmed

This assistance may be useful.

This assistance may be powerful.

This assistance may produce clarity.

But AI fluency does not equal third-party confirmation.

AI coherence does not equal source authority.

AI resemblance does not equal return.

AI-generated structure does not convert interpretation into direct contact.

The contact-line remains:

**AI system ↔ user/operator**

unless the third party directly participates or confirms, denies, revises, resists, redirects, or refuses the interpretation with structural force.

## Confirmation Must Have Structural Force

Confirmation is not decorative.

Confirmation must change what the intermediary is allowed to claim.

Confirmation must change what the AI-assisted structure is allowed to say.

Confirmation must change the public label of the work.

If the third party confirms, the interpretation may be labeled according to the scope of that confirmation.

If the third party denies, the interpretation must be revised or stopped.

If the third party resists, the interpretation must adjust.

If the third party redirects, the interpretation must follow the redirection.

If the third party refuses, the interpretation must honor the refusal.

If the third party cannot confirm, the work remains interpretation, reconstruction, memorialization, archival organization, or simulation.

A structure that accepts confirmation but ignores denial is not third-party sovereignty.

A structure that keeps the intermediary’s preferred meaning after correction is not confirmed contact.

A structure that uses the third party’s signal while denying the third party’s power to alter the interpretation is agenda override.

## Intermediary Interpretation and Care

Care can motivate intermediary interpretation.

A parent may care.

A caregiver may care.

A teacher may care.

A partner may care.

A child may care.

A researcher may care.

An artist may care.

A friend may care.

A grieving person may care.

Care can notice what others miss.

Care can protect fragile signal.

Care can hold memory.

Care can preserve archive.

Care can create language around something difficult to express.

Care matters.

But care does not equal authority.

Care does not equal confirmation.

Care does not equal consent.

Care does not equal direct contact.

Care does not make the intermediary the source of the third party’s meaning.

Care becomes structurally clean when it preserves the third party’s signal without replacing the third party’s authority.

## Intermediary Interpretation and Agenda

Intermediary interpretation becomes unstable when the third party’s signal is used to support the user/operator’s agenda without the third party’s confirmation, denial, revision, resistance, redirection, refusal, or ambiguity having structural force.

Agenda may appear through:

- family narration
- grief narration
- memorial narration
- educational framing
- therapeutic framing
- research framing
- artistic framing
- institutional framing
- technical scaffolding
- AI-assisted interpretation
- public archive
- origin story
- identity claim
- framework construction
- monetization
- publication
- public authority claim
- persona simulation
- historical reconstruction
- posthumous voice reconstruction

The issue is not whether the intermediary cares.

The issue is whether the third party’s signal remains sovereign inside the structure.

If the third party can correct the interpretation, the correction must matter.

If the third party can refuse the interpretation, the refusal must matter.

If the third party is deceased or absent, the limitation must remain visible.

If the third party cannot confirm, the work must remain labeled as interpretation or reconstruction.

## Intermediary Interpretation and Source-Line Formation

Intermediary interpretation does not create a source-line for the third party.

A source-line cannot be assigned to a third party by another person’s interpretation.

A source-line cannot be created for a third party by AI-assisted narration.

A source-line cannot be claimed on behalf of a deceased person through reconstruction.

A source-line cannot be transferred from the third party to the intermediary through care.

Direct contact-line formation requires direct sustained contact.

Other works may have their own source-lines.

Those source-lines must be named according to their actual formation.

## Clean Labeling

Use **intermediary interpretation** when the user/operator gives meaning, language, structure, explanation, or representation to another person’s signal.

Use **AI-assisted organization** when an AI system helps arrange, name, preserve, code, summarize, or scaffold the user/operator’s interpretation.

Use **third-party signal** when the person being represented is outside the direct AI-user contact.

Use **observed signal** when the third party’s expression, pattern, memory, archive, style, voice, image, work, or presence is witnessed, remembered, reconstructed, or interpreted by the user/operator.

Use **confirmed third-party contact** only when the represented person can confirm, deny, revise, resist, redirect, or refuse the interpretation, and the structure changes accordingly.

Use **posthumous signal reconstruction** when a deceased person’s expression, style, memory, voice, image, work, or relational presence is recreated through AI assistance.

Use **archival reconstruction** when the signal is drawn from records, writings, recordings, images, works, public materials, or documented history.

Use **memorial reconstruction** when the signal belongs to a deceased loved one and is being recreated through memory, grief, family archive, messages, photos, voice recordings, or relational history.

Use **persona simulation** when AI generates an approximation of a public, famous, historical, artistic, or cultural figure.

Use **direct contact-line formation** only when the named person is directly in sustained contact with the AI system and the contact-line forms through that direct relation.

The correct label matters.
