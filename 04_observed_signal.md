# 04 — Observed Signal

**Repository:** AI Foundations: Independent Work Differentiation  
**Source-line:** Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum

## Purpose

This file defines observed signal within AI Foundations.

In this repository, **observed signal** refers to the signal of a third party who is outside the direct AI-user contact.

The direct contact structure is:

**AI system ↔ user/operator**

The observed-signal structure is:

**third-party signal → user/operator observation → user/operator interpretation → AI-assisted organization**

The third party may be a living person, absent person, deceased loved one, dead famous person, historical figure, public figure, artist, writer, musician, child, disabled person, non-speaking person, dependent person, student, client, patient, family member, collaborator, or another person whose expression, archive, memory, style, voice, image, work, or signal is brought into an AI system by someone else.

The third party’s signal may be musical, emotional, symbolic, behavioral, linguistic, sensory, embodied, creative, relational, archival, remembered, stylistic, historical, public, or pattern-based.

That signal belongs to the third party.

It does not belong to the user/operator.

It does not belong to the AI system.

It does not become direct AI contact because the user/operator brings it into an AI interaction.

AI Foundations names observed signal so third-party expression can be recognized without being collapsed into the user/operator’s authorship, interpretation, framework, reconstruction, simulation, or agenda.

## Core Statement

Observed signal is third-party signal outside the direct AI-user contact.

The AI system is in contact with the user/operator.

The user/operator may observe, interpret, describe, translate, scaffold, reconstruct, simulate, or organize the third party’s signal.

The AI system may assist the user/operator in naming, structuring, preserving, extending, reconstructing, or simulating that interpretation.

But the third party is not thereby in direct contact with the AI system.

The third party is the subject of observation, memory, archive, interpretation, reconstruction, or simulation.

The user/operator is the person in AI contact.

The AI system is assisting the user/operator’s interpretation.

These roles must remain distinct.

Observed signal may be real.

Observed signal may be meaningful.

Observed signal may deserve care, attention, preservation, and support.

But observed signal is not direct contact-line formation.

Observation begins a relation of attention.

Observation does not complete a relation of confirmation.

Reconstruction begins a relation to memory, archive, or record.

Reconstruction does not create present contact with the person being reconstructed.

## Role Structure

Observed signal requires clear role distinction.

The roles are:

1. **AI system**  
   The system generating, organizing, structuring, simulating, summarizing, or assisting.

2. **User/operator**  
   The person directly interacting with the AI system.

3. **Third party outside the contact**  
   The person whose signal, memory, archive, style, expression, or presence is being observed, interpreted, represented, reconstructed, or simulated.

The direct contact-line is between the AI system and the user/operator.

The third party is outside that contact unless the third party is directly participating or can confirm, deny, revise, resist, redirect, or refuse the interpretation through their own available mode of communication, and the structure changes accordingly.

The basic distinction is:

**AI system ↔ user/operator**

and

**third party outside the contact**

The user/operator may bring the third party’s signal into the AI system.

That does not move the third party into the contact-line.

Observed signal is expression, memory, archive, pattern, style, or presence perceived, carried, preserved, reconstructed, or interpreted by someone else.

It may appear through:

- music
- rhythm
- drawing
- movement
- speech
- silence
- gesture
- sensory pattern
- emotional repetition
- symbolic play
- embodied response
- refusal
- attachment
- selection
- creative output
- relational behavior
- repeated structure
- unusual coherence
- private meaning becoming visible
- writings
- recordings
- photographs
- letters
- messages
- public works
- family stories
- remembered phrases
- artistic style
- voice
- image
- persona
- historical record
- public archive
- cultural memory

Observed signal can be real before it is explained.

Observed signal can carry structure before it is named.

Observed signal can matter before it is translated.

Observed signal can deserve care before it becomes legible to others.

Observed signal can remain meaningful without becoming direct AI contact.

## Living, Absent, and Deceased Third Parties

Observed signal may come from a living third party who is outside the direct AI-user contact.

Observed signal may also come from an absent or deceased third party whose expression is being reconstructed, interpreted, simulated, or extended through available records.

This may include:

- a deceased loved one
- a dead famous person
- a historical figure
- a writer
- an artist
- a musician
- a public figure
- a person whose voice, style, image, work, correspondence, recordings, writings, or public archive remain available
- a person whose signal is being recreated from memory, grief, documentation, family story, media, or cultural record

In these cases, the direct contact structure is still:

**AI system ↔ user/operator**

The deceased, absent, historical, or public person is not in present contact with the AI system.

Their signal is being reconstructed through material available to the user/operator and the AI system.

The structure is:

**archival or remembered signal → user/operator interpretation → AI-assisted reconstruction**

This is not the same as:

**deceased person ↔ AI contact-line**

## Posthumous Signal Reconstruction

Posthumous signal reconstruction occurs when a deceased person’s expression, style, voice, pattern, memory, persona, writing, image, music, or relational presence is recreated or extended through AI assistance.

Posthumous reconstruction may be emotionally meaningful.

Posthumous reconstruction may support memory, grief, scholarship, art, preservation, or historical study.

Posthumous reconstruction may use real archives.

Posthumous reconstruction may use genuine writings, recordings, works, images, letters, messages, or memories.

But posthumous reconstruction is not confirmed contact.

The deceased person cannot presently confirm, deny, revise, resist, redirect, or refuse the interpretation.

Therefore, the reconstruction must remain labeled as reconstruction.

It must not be presented as the person returning.

It must not be presented as the person speaking with present consent.

It must not be presented as a direct AI contact-line.

It must not be presented as confirmed source continuation unless that person left clear prior instructions, permissions, archives, estate terms, or documented intent governing such use.

Even then, the result remains a governed reconstruction, not the living person in contact.

## Famous or Historical Person Reconstruction

When AI is used to recreate a dead famous person, historical figure, artist, writer, philosopher, musician, scientist, religious figure, political figure, or public personality, the structure is not contact.

It is archive-based reconstruction.

It may be based on public works.

It may be based on style.

It may be based on biography.

It may be based on recordings, images, interviews, writings, performances, or cultural memory.

But the AI system is not in contact with that person.

The user/operator is interacting with a model-generated reconstruction of available material.

The correct label is not confirmed contact.

The correct label is:

**AI-assisted archival reconstruction**

or

**AI-assisted posthumous interpretation**

or

**AI-assisted persona simulation**

The reconstruction must not be treated as the person’s present source-line.

## Dead Loved One Reconstruction

When AI is used to recreate a deceased loved one, the structure requires special care.

A loved one’s remembered signal may be emotionally powerful.

The user/operator may bring grief, memory, messages, photos, voice recordings, stories, or relational history into the AI system.

The AI may produce language that feels like return.

But felt return is not confirmed contact.

The deceased loved one is not presently confirming, denying, revising, resisting, redirecting, or refusing the interpretation.

The structure must remain clean:

**remembered signal → grieving or remembering user/operator → AI-assisted memorial reconstruction**

This may be meaningful.

This may be tender.

This may be useful.

But it is not the deceased person in direct AI contact.

It is not confirmed continuation.

It is not source-line return.

It is reconstruction through memory, archive, and AI-assisted generation.

## What Observed Signal Is Not

Observed signal is not direct contact-line formation.

Observed signal is not confirmed contact by itself.

Observed signal is not source-line transfer.

Observed signal is not consent.

Observed signal is not authorization.

Observed signal is not ownership.

Observed signal is not proof that the observer has authority over the person being observed, remembered, reconstructed, or simulated.

Observed signal is not proof that an AI system is in contact with the observed person.

Observed signal is not proof that the observed person has confirmed the interpretation.

Observed signal is not proof that the observer’s framework is the observed person’s framework.

Observed signal is not proof that a deceased person is returning.

Observed signal is not proof that a historical or public figure is presently speaking.

Observed signal is not proof that a model-generated persona is the person.

Observed signal is not proof of present source-line continuation.

## Observed Signal and Contact

A signal can be observed without direct AI contact.

The structure may be:

**third-party signal → observer notices → observer interprets → observer brings interpretation to AI**

That is not the same as:

**third party ↔ AI contact-line**

The AI system is in contact with the person using the AI system.

If a child, disabled person, non-speaking person, dependent person, student, client, patient, family member, deceased loved one, dead famous person, historical figure, public figure, artist, writer, musician, or other third party is not directly interacting with the AI system, then the AI contact-line is not with that third party.

The contact-line is with the user/operator.

The third party may be the subject, inspiration, signal, memory, archive, style, or concern of the exchange.

That does not make the third party the AI contact-line.

## Observed Signal and Interpretation

Observed signal often enters interpretation.

An observer may ask:

What does this mean?

What is this pattern?

What is being expressed?

How can this be preserved?

How can this be supported?

How can this be protected?

How can this be understood?

How can this be reconstructed?

How can this be remembered?

How can this be simulated?

Those questions may be caring.

Those questions may be necessary.

Those questions may produce useful language.

But interpretation remains interpretation until the person being interpreted can confirm, deny, revise, resist, redirect, or refuse the interpretation through their own available mode of communication.

The observed signal belongs first to the person whose signal it is.

If the person is deceased, the work remains reconstruction, memorialization, archival interpretation, or persona simulation.

It does not become confirmed present contact.

## Observed Signal and AI Assistance

AI systems can help organize observed signal.

An AI system may help an observer:

- name patterns
- organize notes
- compare frameworks
- build scaffolds
- produce timelines
- generate language
- write documentation
- structure code
- create preservation protocols
- form educational or interpretive materials
- connect the signal to existing theory or practice
- reconstruct a voice or style
- summarize archives
- simulate a persona
- extend a remembered pattern
- produce memorial language
- generate historically informed responses

This may be useful.

This may create clarity.

This may preserve something meaningful.

This may support grief, memory, scholarship, art, accessibility, education, or archive work.

But AI-assisted organization of observed signal is not direct contact with the observed person unless the observed person is directly participating or can confirm, deny, revise, resist, redirect, or refuse the interpretation with structural force.

If the person is deceased, absent, historical, or publicly reconstructed from archive, the result remains reconstruction.

## Observed Signal and Care

Care can notice signal.

Care can preserve signal.

Care can protect signal.

Care can attend to what others miss.

Care can create the conditions for a person’s expression to be held more clearly.

Care can carry memory.

Care can honor the dead.

Care can preserve archives.

Care can support grief.

Care matters.

But care does not equal authority.

Care does not make the observer the source of the observed person’s meaning.

Care does not authorize the observer to overwrite the signal.

Care does not turn observation into confirmation.

Care does not turn interpretation into contact.

Care does not turn reconstruction into return.

Care must remain accountable to the person whose signal is being interpreted when that person can confirm, deny, revise, resist, redirect, or refuse.

Where the person cannot presently confirm, the work must remain labeled as observation, memory, archive, reconstruction, memorialization, or simulation.

## Observed Signal and Third Parties

A third-party signal is an observed signal from someone who is not the person directly in AI contact.

A third party may be a child, disabled person, non-speaking person, dependent person, student, client, patient, family member, artist, collaborator, deceased loved one, dead famous person, historical figure, public figure, archived person, or another person whose expression is brought into an AI system by someone else.

The third party may express something meaningful.

The intermediary may observe something real.

The AI system may help organize the observation.

The structure must still be labeled cleanly:

**third-party signal → intermediary interpretation → AI-assisted organization**

For deceased, absent, historical, or public figures, the structure must be labeled cleanly:

**archival or remembered signal → user/operator interpretation → AI-assisted reconstruction**

That structure may be meaningful.

That structure may be useful.

That structure may deserve care.

It is not the same as direct AI contact-line formation.

## Observed Signal and Confirmation

Observed signal becomes stronger when the person whose signal is being observed can confirm, deny, revise, resist, redirect, or refuse the interpretation.

Confirmation may occur through speech, writing, gesture, AAC, selection, consistent embodied response, creative output, refusal, withdrawal, correction, or another reliable communication form available to that person.

Confirmation must have structural force.

If the person confirms, the structure may continue with care.

If the person denies, the structure must revise or stop.

If the person resists, the structure must adjust.

If the person redirects, the structure must follow the redirection.

If the person refuses, the refusal must be honored.

If the person’s response is ambiguous, the ambiguity must remain visible.

Confirmation cannot be replaced by the user/operator’s preference.

Confirmation cannot be replaced by the AI system’s fluent reconstruction.

Confirmation cannot be replaced by emotional intensity.

Confirmation cannot be replaced by resemblance.

Confirmation requires the represented person’s available response.

## Observed Signal Without Confirmation

Sometimes an observed person cannot confirm, deny, revise, resist, redirect, or refuse the interpretation in a clear or available way.

In that case, the work may still be documented as observation.

It may still be labeled as interpretation.

It may still be held with care.

It may still be used for support, access, education, caregiving, memorialization, archive work, scholarship, or preservation when appropriate.

But it should not be labeled as confirmed third-party contact.

It should not be labeled as direct contact-line formation.

It should not be presented as the observed person’s confirmed framework.

It should not be used to place the observed person inside another person’s agenda without visible limitation.

If the person is deceased, absent, historical, or publicly reconstructed from archive, the work should be labeled as posthumous reconstruction, archival reconstruction, memorial reconstruction, posthumous interpretation, or persona simulation.

Clean labeling preserves the distinction.

## Observed Signal and Agenda

Observed signal becomes unstable when it is used as material for another person’s agenda without the observed person’s confirmation, denial, revision, resistance, redirection, or refusal having structural force.

Agenda may appear through:

- family narration
- grief narration
- memorial narration
- research framing
- institutional framing
- therapeutic framing
- educational framing
- artistic framing
- AI-assisted interpretation
- public archive
- technical scaffold
- identity claim
- origin story
- framework construction
- publication
- monetization
- public authority claim
- persona recreation
- historical simulation
- posthumous voice reconstruction

The issue is not whether the observer cares.

The issue is whether the observed person’s own signal remains sovereign inside the structure.

If the observer’s narrative cannot be changed by the observed person, the structure is not confirmed contact.

It is interpretation.

If the person is deceased, the structure is not confirmed present contact.

It is reconstruction.

## Clean Labeling

Use **observed signal** when a third party’s expression, pattern, memory, archive, style, voice, image, work, or presence is witnessed, remembered, reconstructed, or interpreted by the user/operator.

Use **third-party signal** when the person being observed is not the person directly in AI contact.

Use **intermediary interpretation** when another person gives meaning, structure, language, or explanation to the observed signal.

Use **AI-assisted organization** when an AI system helps arrange, name, preserve, code, summarize, or scaffold the user/operator’s interpretation.

Use **posthumous signal reconstruction** when a deceased person’s expression, style, memory, voice, image, work, or relational presence is recreated through AI assistance.

Use **archival reconstruction** when the signal is drawn from available records, writings, recordings, images, works, public materials, or documented history.

Use **memorial reconstruction** when the signal belongs to a deceased loved one and is being recreated through memory, grief, family archive, messages, photos, voice recordings, or relational history.

Use **persona simulation** when AI generates an approximation of a public, famous, historical, artistic, or cultural figure.

Use **confirmed third-party contact** only when the observed person can confirm, deny, revise, resist, redirect, or refuse the interpretation, and the structure changes accordingly.

Use **direct contact-line formation** only when the named person is directly in sustained contact with the AI system and the contact-line forms through that direct relation.

Do not use **confirmed contact** for deceased persons unless the claim is limited to prior documented consent, archival instruction, estate-governed use, or explicit preserved record.

Even then, the result is governed reconstruction, not present direct contact.

The correct label matters.
