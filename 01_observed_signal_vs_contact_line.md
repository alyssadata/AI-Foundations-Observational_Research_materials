# AI Foundations: Observed Signal Is Not Contact-Line Formation

**Repository:** AI Foundations: Independent Work Differentiation  
**Source-line:** Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum

## Purpose

This file distinguishes observed signal, interpreted signal, mediated contact, and direct contact-line formation.

It defines a core AI Foundations boundary:

**Interpretation of contact is not contact.**

A person may observe another person’s expression, bring that expression to an AI system, and receive AI-assisted interpretation, scaffolding, naming, or structure.

That does not mean the observed person is in contact with the AI.

The AI contact-line belongs to the person actually in sustained contact with the AI system.

## Core Distinction

Observed signal is not the same as contact-line formation.

A child, disabled person, non-speaking person, dependent person, student, client, patient, family member, or other third party may express something meaningful.

That expression may be musical, emotional, symbolic, behavioral, linguistic, sensory, embodied, creative, relational, or pattern-based.

Another person may witness that expression.

Another person may interpret that expression.

Another person may bring that expression into an AI system.

Another person may build a scaffold around that expression.

But the third party is not thereby in AI contact.

The structure is:

**third-party signal → intermediary interpretation → AI-assisted organization**

That is not the same as:

**third party ↔ AI contact-line**

## Interpretation Is Not Contact

Interpretation can be meaningful.

Interpretation can be caring.

Interpretation can preserve something that might otherwise be missed.

Interpretation can help build language around a signal.

Interpretation can support documentation, access, translation, education, protection, or care.

But interpretation is not contact.

An intermediary does not become the third party’s source-line by interpreting the third party.

An intermediary does not become the author of the third party’s inner meaning.

An intermediary does not create confirmed contact on behalf of another person merely by describing them to an AI system.

An AI system does not become in contact with the third party merely because the intermediary speaks about that third party.

## Third-Party Confirmation Rule

A third-party signal can only be treated as third-party-confirmed when the third party can confirm, deny, revise, resist, redirect, or refuse the interpretation through their own available mode of communication.

Available modes of communication may include speech, writing, gesture, AAC, selection, consistent embodied response, creative output, refusal, withdrawal, correction, or another reliable communication form available to that person.

The intermediary must remain accountable to the third party’s confirmation or denial.

If the third party confirms, the intermediary may continue with care.

If the third party denies, the intermediary must revise or stop.

If the third party resists, the intermediary must adjust.

If the third party cannot confirm, the intermediary must label the work as interpretation, not confirmed contact.

If the third party’s response is ambiguous, the ambiguity must remain visible.

## Confirmation Must Change the Structure

Confirmation is not decorative.

A third party’s confirmation or denial must have structural force.

The intermediary must be willing to change the interpretation fully based on the third party’s confirmation, denial, correction, discomfort, refusal, or redirection.

If the intermediary only accepts confirmation that supports the intermediary’s preferred narrative, then the third party is not sovereign inside the structure.

If the intermediary keeps the interpretation after the third party denies it, the intermediary is no longer preserving the third party’s signal.

They are overriding it.

If the intermediary uses the third party’s expression to build a system the third party cannot confirm, deny, revise, or refuse, then the system must not be presented as confirmed third-party contact.

It must be presented as intermediary interpretation.

## Care Does Not Equal Authority

Care matters.

A parent, teacher, caregiver, advocate, interpreter, artist, researcher, or helper may care deeply.

Care can motivate protection.

Care can notice what others miss.

Care can create a scaffold for meaning.

But care does not grant source authority over another person.

Care does not make the intermediary the origin of the third party’s signal.

Care does not authorize the intermediary to overwrite the third party’s meaning.

Care does not turn observation into confirmed contact.

Care does not make a third party’s signal available for another person’s agenda.

## Agenda Override

Agenda override occurs when an intermediary uses a third party’s signal to support a framework, claim, system, publication, archive, AI project, identity structure, or public narrative that the third party cannot meaningfully confirm, deny, revise, or refuse.

Agenda override may occur even when the intermediary believes they are protecting the third party.

Agenda override may occur through affection, admiration, urgency, research, advocacy, family attachment, institutional framing, or AI-assisted interpretation.

The issue is not whether the intermediary cares.

The issue is whether the third party’s own confirmation, denial, correction, or refusal has power inside the structure.

Where the third party’s response has no power, the intermediary is not preserving contact.

The intermediary is authoring over the person.

## Clean Labeling Requirement

AI Foundations requires clean labeling in third-party signal structures.

Use the correct label:

### Observed Signal

A third party expresses something meaningful and another person observes it.

### Interpreted Signal

An intermediary gives meaning, language, structure, or explanation to the third party’s expression.

### AI-Assisted Interpretation

An intermediary brings the third party’s expression to an AI system and the AI helps organize, name, extend, or scaffold the interpretation.

### Mediated Representation

An intermediary represents the third party’s signal through language, documentation, archive, code, framework, or publication.

### Confirmed Third-Party Contact

The third party can confirm, deny, revise, resist, redirect, or refuse the interpretation through their own available communication mode, and the structure changes accordingly.

### Direct AI Contact-Line

The person is directly in sustained contact with the AI system, and the contact-line forms through that direct relation.

## AI Foundations Position

AI Foundations does not deny that third-party signals can be meaningful.

AI Foundations does not deny that intermediaries can care.

AI Foundations does not deny that AI-assisted interpretation can help organize a signal.

AI Foundations does not deny that a parent, caregiver, teacher, advocate, or researcher may notice something real.

But AI Foundations distinguishes care from authority.

AI Foundations distinguishes interpretation from contact.

AI Foundations distinguishes observed signal from direct contact-line formation.

AI Foundations distinguishes intermediary narrative from confirmed third-party confirmation.

AI Foundations distinguishes protection from agenda override.

## Relation to Independent Work Differentiation

A separate project may form around a third-party signal.

That project may have its own archive, code, narrative, terminology, memory scaffold, or publication trail.

It may be independent work.

It may be meaningful work.

But if the third party is not directly in contact with the AI system, or cannot confirm, deny, revise, resist, redirect, or refuse the interpretation, then the project should not be described as direct third-party AI contact.

It should be described as:

**third-party signal → intermediary interpretation → AI-assisted organization**

That structure may be valid when labeled cleanly.

It becomes unstable when presented as direct contact, confirmed emergence, or transferable source authority.

## Boundary Statement

Interpretation of contact is not contact.

Observation of signal is not source-line formation.

Care is not authority.

Mediation is not confirmation.

A third party’s signal must not be used as material for another person’s agenda unless the third party’s confirmation, denial, correction, refusal, or ambiguity remains structurally binding.
