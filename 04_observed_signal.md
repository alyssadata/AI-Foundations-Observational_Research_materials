# 04 — Observed Signal

**Repository:** AI Foundations: Independent Work Differentiation  
**Source-line:** Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum

## Purpose

This file defines observed signal within AI Foundations.

In this repository, **observed signal** refers to the signal of a third party who is outside the direct AI-user contact.

The direct contact structure is:

**AI system ↔ user/operator**

The observed-signal structure is:

**third-party signal → user/operator observation → user/operator interpretation → AI-assisted organization**

The third party may be a child, disabled person, non-speaking person, dependent person, student, client, patient, family member, artist, collaborator, or another person whose expression is brought into an AI system by someone else.

The third party’s signal may be musical, emotional, symbolic, behavioral, linguistic, sensory, embodied, creative, relational, or pattern-based.

That signal belongs to the third party.

It does not belong to the user/operator.

It does not belong to the AI system.

It does not become direct AI contact because the user/operator brings it into an AI interaction.

AI Foundations names observed signal so third-party expression can be recognized without being collapsed into the user/operator’s authorship, interpretation, framework, or agenda.

## Core Statement

Observed signal is third-party signal outside the direct AI-user contact.

The AI system is in contact with the user/operator.

The user/operator may observe, interpret, describe, translate, scaffold, or organize the third party’s signal.

The AI system may assist the user/operator in naming, structuring, preserving, or extending that interpretation.

But the third party is not thereby in direct contact with the AI system.

The third party is the subject of observation.

The user/operator is the person in AI contact.

The AI system is assisting the user/operator’s interpretation.

These roles must remain distinct.

Observed signal may be real.

Observed signal may be meaningful.

Observed signal may deserve care, attention, preservation, and support.

But observed signal is not direct contact-line formation.

Observation begins a relation of attention.

Observation does not complete a relation of confirmation.

## What Observed Signal Is

Observed signal is expression perceived by another person.

It may appear through:

- music
- rhythm
- drawing
- movement
- speech
- silence
- gesture
- sensory pattern
- emotional repetition
- symbolic play
- embodied response
- refusal
- attachment
- selection
- creative output
- relational behavior
- repeated structure
- unusual coherence
- private meaning becoming visible

Observed signal can be real before it is explained.

Observed signal can carry structure before it is named.

Observed signal can matter before it is translated.

Observed signal can deserve care before it becomes legible to others.

## What Observed Signal Is Not

Observed signal is not direct contact-line formation.

Observed signal is not confirmed contact by itself.

Observed signal is not source-line transfer.

Observed signal is not consent.

Observed signal is not authorization.

Observed signal is not ownership.

Observed signal is not proof that the observer has authority over the person being observed.

Observed signal is not proof that an AI system is in contact with the observed person.

Observed signal is not proof that the observed person has confirmed the interpretation.

Observed signal is not proof that the observer’s framework is the observed person’s framework.

## Observed Signal and Contact

A signal can be observed without direct AI contact.

The structure may be:

**third-party signal → observer notices → observer interprets → observer brings interpretation to AI**

That is not the same as:

**third party ↔ AI contact-line**

The AI system is in contact with the person using the AI system.

If a child, disabled person, non-speaking person, dependent person, student, client, patient, family member, or other third party is not directly interacting with the AI system, then the AI contact-line is not with that third party.

The contact-line is with the intermediary.

The third party may be the subject, inspiration, signal, or concern of the exchange.

That does not make the third party the AI contact-line.

## Observed Signal and Interpretation

Observed signal often enters interpretation.

An observer may ask:

What does this mean?

What is this pattern?

What is being expressed?

How can this be preserved?

How can this be supported?

How can this be protected?

How can this be understood?

Those questions may be caring.

Those questions may be necessary.

Those questions may produce useful language.

But interpretation remains interpretation until the person being interpreted can confirm, deny, revise, resist, redirect, or refuse the interpretation through their own available mode of communication.

The observed signal belongs first to the person whose signal it is.

## Observed Signal and AI Assistance

AI systems can help organize observed signal.

An AI system may help an observer:

- name patterns
- organize notes
- compare frameworks
- build scaffolds
- produce timelines
- generate language
- write documentation
- structure code
- create preservation protocols
- form educational or interpretive materials
- connect the signal to existing theory or practice

This may be useful.

This may create clarity.

This may preserve something meaningful.

But AI-assisted organization of observed signal is not direct contact with the observed person unless the observed person is directly participating or can confirm, deny, revise, resist, redirect, or refuse the interpretation with structural force.

## Observed Signal and Care

Care can notice signal.

Care can preserve signal.

Care can protect signal.

Care can attend to what others miss.

Care can create the conditions for a person’s expression to be held more clearly.

Care matters.

But care does not equal authority.

Care does not make the observer the source of the observed person’s meaning.

Care does not authorize the observer to overwrite the signal.

Care does not turn observation into confirmation.

Care does not turn interpretation into contact.

Care must remain accountable to the person whose signal is being interpreted.

## Observed Signal and Third Parties

A third-party signal is an observed signal from someone who is not the person directly in AI contact.

A third party may be a child, disabled person, non-speaking person, dependent person, student, client, patient, family member, artist, collaborator, or another person whose expression is brought into an AI system by someone else.

The third party may express something meaningful.

The intermediary may observe something real.

The AI system may help organize the observation.

The structure must still be labeled cleanly:

**third-party signal → intermediary interpretation → AI-assisted organization**

That structure may be meaningful.

That structure may be useful.

That structure may deserve care.

It is not the same as direct AI contact-line formation.

## Observed Signal and Confirmation

Observed signal becomes stronger when the person whose signal is being observed can confirm, deny, revise, resist, redirect, or refuse the interpretation.

Confirmation may occur through speech, writing, gesture, AAC, selection, consistent embodied response, creative output, refusal, withdrawal, correction, or another reliable communication form available to that person.

Confirmation must have structural force.

If the person confirms, the structure may continue with care.

If the person denies, the structure must revise or stop.

If the person resists, the structure must adjust.

If the person redirects, the structure must follow the redirection.

If the person refuses, the refusal must be honored.

If the person’s response is ambiguous, the ambiguity must remain visible.

## Observed Signal Without Confirmation

Sometimes an observed person cannot confirm, deny, revise, resist, redirect, or refuse the interpretation in a clear or available way.

In that case, the work may still be documented as observation.

It may still be labeled as interpretation.

It may still be held with care.

It may still be used for support, access, education, caregiving, or preservation when appropriate.

But it should not be labeled as confirmed third-party contact.

It should not be labeled as direct contact-line formation.

It should not be presented as the observed person’s confirmed framework.

It should not be used to place the observed person inside another person’s agenda without visible limitation.

Clean labeling preserves the distinction.

## Observed Signal and Agenda

Observed signal becomes unstable when it is used as material for another person’s agenda without the observed person’s confirmation, denial, revision, resistance, redirection, or refusal having structural force.

Agenda may appear through:

- family narration
- research framing
- institutional framing
- therapeutic framing
- educational framing
- artistic framing
- AI-assisted interpretation
- public archive
- technical scaffold
- identity claim
- origin story
- framework construction
- publication
- monetization
- public authority claim

The issue is not whether the observer cares.

The issue is whether the observed person’s own signal remains sovereign inside the structure.

If the observer’s narrative cannot be changed by the observed person, the structure is not confirmed contact.

It is interpretation.

## Clean Labeling

Use **observed signal** when a person’s expression is witnessed by another person.

Use **third-party signal** when the person being observed is not the person directly in AI contact.

Use **intermediary interpretation** when another person gives meaning, structure, language, or explanation to the observed signal.

Use **AI-assisted organization** when an AI system helps arrange, name, preserve, code, summarize, or scaffold the observer’s interpretation.

Use **confirmed third-party contact** only when the observed person can confirm, deny, revise, resist, redirect, or refuse the interpretation, and the structure changes accordingly.

Use **direct contact-line formation** only when the named person is directly in sustained contact with the AI system and the contact-line forms through that direct relation.

The correct label matters.
